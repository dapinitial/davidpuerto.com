#hackHousing Winner.

Managed with Full-stack UX Design


for #hackHousing hosted at Zillow



Judged by: Data.gov, US Census Bureau, U.S. Department of Commerce, the Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD), Zillow, Socrata, Seattle Housing Authority, and The University of Washington



"Empowering Smarter Decisions"



FEBRUARY 8-9, 2015 / SEATTLE, WA



Created "SmartMove" while competing against 400+ individuals (including Zillow HotPads NYC/SF employees) comprising ~40 teams of technology professionals in one of the largest hackathons in Seattle history. Awarded first-place by a panel of judges from Zillow, HUD, data.gov, and the U.S. Census Bureau, won $10,000, and graced the front page of GeekWire landing amongst their "must reads" category.


Spawned to action having owned a home since 2008 and making all the wrong choices buying a house I had a plan. I sprang into action and gathered the data that I needed querying the available resources from the various agencies and exchanged personal contact information with people who had tribal knowledge to continue to work fast the way I was working at Facebook on FBIDE. We left the venue and nestled into fighting force whiteboarding and lacing some proof of concepts to deal with the data.


The next 24 hours we pretty much sank into our roles where I was the UX Designer, Visual Designer, and Presentation Layer Developer. Using a variety of jQuery plugins was able to turn our entire app into what appeared to be a touch screen projector application that allowed swiping and selecting. "Fake it till you make it" prototyping madness while my engineers delivered VALUE in ACTUAL data that was being returned.


To factor our application response off a needs-analysis based on personal and financial factors that the user input doing the configurator process during first and subsequent runs via a User Settings Menu. Along with where people work and spend their time. As you build your "habitat" points on a map are returned representing housing options that match your needs and have federal and local grants and assistance programs that you qualify for. You can add these documents to a queue to fill out and submit later essentially pushing people to become homeowners and in most cases saving them from having to come out of their own pockets for a down payment! (This part of the application was prototyped from the actual data gathered from users and actual results that matched them to locations and properties and grants and other assistance options available).
