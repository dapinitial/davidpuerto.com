Codename: Lyric

Case Study

Contracted as the UI Designer to research the current FBIDE product and identify pain points and areas to enhance the UI to enhance the overall user experience and increase discoverability while simultaneously creating visual design assets, a look and feel, syntax and UI themes, and finally implementation.

I first tasked myself to reach out to the users! I ran queries to determine who were the most active users and then ran queries over the last twelve months to determine who had been an active user that was no longer using FBIDE to determine where we were failing and how we could win them back. This was an incredibly fun project to begin as FBIDE has ~1000 active users who are internally using the cloud-based IDE to develop and debug code on the HHVM in Hack and best of all languages are supported. FBIDE is demoed to boot campers and interns alike. I found that many people when I started the project had tried it out and switched back to their preferred methods of programming. Upon inquiry I learned that many VIM and eMacs users were going to be hard to convert... unless of course we have VIM and VIM-style key mapping!! So we entered that as a task and delievered! Observing the entire Facebook developer life cycle I was able to extract the basis from which move developers begin and enter and continue and finish programs and their overall workflow including expected feedback loops. Having an active user base and users who were active in our internal Facebook group made for an amazing UX experience querying users and responding to comments. When I started to implement some of the changes literally hundreds of posts were made with such excitement for the enhanced UI experience. I've never been on a project where the users were currently dog fooding the product and it definitely enhanced the outcome by ensuring that every step we made was in the right direction with our users Q&Aing the product for us so we could establish what was working and what was not.

The work from Lyric is now being ported over to another open-source IDE and being co-developed internally with another group called `Nuclide` that I am currently on contract with.Today the entire experience is serving as a living prototype that the users are still using while we put the polish on Nuclide being demoed at F8.
