    SmartGlass 2.0 UX & Prototype

    Explored Xbox SmartGlass 2.0's User Experience to provide high-fidelity prototypes via web technologies maintaining a single codebase for cross-platform testing.

    Prototyped and implemented designs for HRWeb with ARIA-tags for inclusion. Prototyped a filtering system to locate and communicate with employees who had bandwidth and expertise based on #hashtag filters that were in place on Dr. Who, the interactive org chart.

    My introduction to Microsoft began at Xbox SmartGlass during Durango leading up to the Xbox One. I am totally a spoiled brat.After interviewing with a guy who didn't say much but asked me about my experiences with tablets, smartphones, and responsive design I explained my ceiling to him with JavaScript and what I could and couldn't do and how I solved problems.  Towards the end he admitted to being "near perfect at JavaScript" and he probably pretty much is, as well as being an awesome Objective-C programmer and resident iOS developer. Huge inspiration for someone who has been sort of absent of good front-end coding guidance for nearly his entire career.

    I began sitting in with the SmartGlass 2.0 UX team and listening to what their thoughts were and offering up some usability and navigational ideas of my own.   Rapid prototyping ensued and clickable wireframe prototypes were in their hands almost as soon as I'd started viewable on iOS, Droid, and Windows Phone 8 (which I actually became rather fond of).  From there higher fidelity prototypes were offered up from actual motion and visual design comps and decisions were made, yes, this will work, or no, we'll need to massage this one out.

    My favorite part of this project, aside from the way lax Microsoft culture, playing Fruit Ninjas, and doing daily standups that were more like peps talks, and all the hip-hop music blasting from the speakers was whiteboarding with UX designers and jumping right into building stuff, working with motion designers to get my cubic beziers, and working with the Dynamic Backstack via push() and pop() and having a back button that worked like an internal history button and swiping back to a certain point and the nav Drawer opening.

    Extremely sharp UX team. Unfortunately, since they built SmartGlass in native code, once their explorations, discovery, and decisions were made to proceed they didn't need a prototyper hanging around, but for me it was genuinely worthwhile and the exposure to how some of the brightest approach problem solving and how I could leverage my skillset to do more than just presentation layer web development and design was more than paying my contract rate could ever offer.  Awesome experience, they walked me out with smiles and a handshake, and I was able to enter this secret room in Studio A to play Durango in a dark gray room with a baddass cow hide ottoman that you could plug controllers into!  I even found Bill Gates's business card in their Post Alley Cafe!

    February 8-9, 2015 / Redmond, WA
