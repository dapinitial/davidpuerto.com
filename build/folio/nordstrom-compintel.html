The problem you are trying to solve

— What can’t they do now or can’t do well that your design is meant to address?


Current "Competitive intelligence" price matching workflows involve several wells of individually-held tribal knowledge covering dozens of non-standardized process flows that ultimately culminate in the execution of macros to do some of the calculation and heavy lifting and eventual entry into RMS. That execution has a long processing time into RMS 9 which provides a major opportunity to deliver a web-based application with a measurable gain in perforance and processing time. To offload some of the burdens our QA’s and Buying Office experience every day by improving performance and simplyfying their workflow we proposed an entirely new workflow with CompIntel detailed below.



Users — [Admins, QAs, Buying Office, SuperAdmins]


Quality Assurance Analysts (QA’s) and The Buying Office (BO's) spend countless hours each day running formulas in Excel and emailing each other back and forth as their general workflow. The third-party data [from Boomerang] is not always accurate and current process is intense and laborious in an already time-sensitive scenario (as the often-inaccurate Boomerang data refreshes before QAs can mitigate the thousands of items that must be manually clicked and checked including all size and color permutations that competitor retailers may host, rendering it untrustworthy). We are serving the customer by providing a more collaborative environment to perform their daily tasks and an expedited workflow with our amalgamation of tools to aggregate a massive amount of information into a succinct workflow that includes QA and BO interfaces, daily assignments, cadence, default cadence, admin, and super administrative task handling. Our eventual plan will be to remove the dependency on Boomerang saving millions of dollars and freeing up budgets for the company and “eventually building a robot that does it all.” A future enhancement (mid-late 2019) combines our tool with a companion application, a Chrome extension, to further expedite the QA validation process by guiding the QA and BO through the verification stage off-site instructing them where to click so we can prop-drill for all the sizes and colors and automate the process of verifying scores of otherwise buried data that requires manual clicks are sometimes dozens or hundreds of colors and size permutations article or item dependent. From this initial platform we seek to expand upon the infrastructure to provide the foundation for future ML/AI efforts and deliver a platform to connect previously disjointed aspects of the enterprise through this modernization effort.


This tool begins by enabling the delegation of the week’s work based on a pre-determined Division Priority and rotation of users. Using a single-entry point, we provide a roles-based division of labor between QA and BO where they perform the manual verification/modification of [untrustworthy and expensive] Boomerang Data that ensures data accuracy when setting the price (retail matching). Each QA is responsible for a swath of work that an administrator delegates via a drag and drop interface that tracks the progress and returns a feedback loop of graphical data. A single QA-person could be responsible for thousands of items that need to be QA’d in a given time frame. QA-people check other retailers by manually searching for items based on Boomerang Data to determinate that price, size, color, etc are accurate. Our tool provides all of this information in a single view vs. numerous spreadsheets. Items can be self-delegated, searched, reorganized, filtered, acted upon, etc inside our tool. The Nordstrom Item and the Other Retailer items are quickly referenceable and QA can begin the validation process immediately inside the tool. When they have confirmed matches, and made any necessary adjustments, each validated item is sent off to the Buying Office for review. BO confirms the matches provided by QA and puts a clearance plan into action. Admins determine the Users and Roles, the Labels and Categories for departments and divisions, and then assign those departments and divisions to users based on roles to then delegate and determine a cadence for the week’s work using a drag and drop interface.


Because we are passing state throughout the app we are able to capture all of the users' events and have a glimpse into everything that they are clicking, when they are clicking, where they are clicking, and their order of operations, and the time it takes to complete tasks, etc. Bottlenecks and painpoints will hopefully be surfaced and additional UX work can be performed to enhance our users' operation of the tool.



Measurable Results
— How did the design work improve the user experience and benefit the business? —

Prior to the inception of CompIntel execution status for 3/4 of the users was below the target execution threshold of 80%. After CompIntel was unleashed: 100% of the users reached 100% execution status. Much higher than the target threshold of 80%!






This is our beta approach twards developing a more robust system of automation and continuing to build tools to harness the power that our users have within them. CompIntel presented the first opportunity to learn from our new modernization effort and build towards the CommonUI as this was a ground-up effort from the inside out. We completely owned the engineer and design from end to end in house. I have ideas to incentivize them to become more productive via gamification. She thought it was super cool. She's our most powerful user and the person who shared her workflow with us and gave us the most insight into what we needed to develop.
