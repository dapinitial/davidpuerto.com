David Puerto

UX Engineer / UI Designer / CSS Specialist

About

Over the last decade David Puerto has established a career through the mastery of meaningful, interactive media design. Dedicated to creating refined, yet innovative ecosystems, David Puerto synthesizes complex workflows into extensible UI pattern libraries expressing the underlying design language. Experienced in web-based screen design and nimble along the modern web development toolchain; he prides himself as a seasoned, hands-on UX leader that delivers the highest-level of fit and finish -- thriving on props from brainchild to launch.

Coming up in the days of Flash I was heavily immersed in interaction and time-based animation along with thinking in terms of scalability and portability. I chose Flash because at the time it was the go-to solution for cross-browser websites with 90% of computers running Flash by default. Along with vector graphics and some ActionScript, Flash was the no-brainer tool for the rich-interactive web experiences of the early and mid-2000s. With the advent of smart devices and tablets I've chosen to fall back to traditional web technologies but I still think in terms of how I can deliver the same scalable, device agnostic experience that I strove for with Flash.

Today I am looking beyond the web as design technology becomes standard in our homes and in our lives. What can I dream up that can help make everyone's lives simpler so that we spend less time hooking things up and more time enjoying the fruits of our labors? You'll see. My hopes is you'll enjoy using it too.

My Methodology

Prototyping
Design in the Browser

I practice a LEAN style of UX, close to the metal, working alongside engineers and checking in with design teams for review as iterate over ideas by creating failing fast and filling in those pitfalls often. Prototypes allow for rapidly producing proofs of concept that are functional where UI decisions can be tested and verified. HTML serves as my information architecture, my wireframe, the place where I frame the MVP - that's why it was created. CSS and JavaScript helps craft design around technology enabling me to craft an experience by observing how it all lives together in its natural ecosystem. Oftentimes we make discoveries early on in our prototyping that lend to a better overall user experience and becomes a creative solution we otherwise may have missed designing a static document, especially when designing for big data problems where we might need to test 100 columns and 100000 rows of data. You cannot mock that up in static design software. You can't. You wouldn't even try. Right tool for the right job.

Visual Design

Whether it's Sketch, Photoshop, Illustrator, or Flash, I've always loved pushing the limits of what I can do and applying the gloss. Thinking in terms of how it will be assembled, all the way through to how the user will actually be holding it in their hand or using the mouse, ensures that each decision I make is conscientious of our platform. I've always been an admirer of clean, sleek, and dark interfaces that feel very future is now.

Design Implementation

This is where I get my hands dirty and put it all together with HTML5 and CSS3, the gold standard for web design. Being able to demonstrate how the end product should look and feel also lends the added benefit of helping out with engineering and hopefully carrying over into a harmonious work environment. Oftentimes there are things that I can do with CSS that would typically take hundreds of lines of a classical programming language to accomplish and when I can be an asset to engineering and get applause for a clever solution that saves lots of engineering overhead I feel great satisfaction.
