Make the Smart Move

#hackHousing Winner

Case Study

Alongside my team, SpaceLabForever, created "SmartMove" while competing against 200+ individuals (including Zillow HotPads NYC/SF employees) comprising ~40 teams of technology professionals in one of the largest hackathons in Seattle history. Awarded first-place by a panel of judges from Zillow, HUD, data.gov, and the U.S. Census Bureau, won $10,000, and graced the front page of GeekWire landing amongst their "must reads" category.

Spawned to action having owned a home since 2008 and making all the wrong choices buying a house I had a plan. I sprang into action and gathered the data that I needed querying the available resources from the various agencies and exchanged personal contact information. We left the venue and nestled into fighting force whiteboarding and lacing some proof of concepts to deal with the data.

The next 24 hours we pretty much sank into our roles where I was the UX Designer, Visual Designer, and Presentation Layer Developer. Using a variety of jQuery plugins was able to turn our entire app into what appeared to be a touch screen projector application that allowed swiping and selecting.

Based on user input we were able to determine a needs analysis based on personal and financial factors along with where people work and spend their time. As you build your "habitat" points on a map are returned representing housing options that match your needs and have federal and local grants and assistance programs that you qualify for. You can add these documents to a queue to fill out and submit later essentially pushing people to become homeowners and in most cases saving them from having to come out of their own pockets for a down payment!
